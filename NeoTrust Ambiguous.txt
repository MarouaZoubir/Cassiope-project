=== DOCUMENT 1 : Information Security Statement (Extended) ===
Version: 2.0 – Oct 2025
Language: English
Owner: Head of Security & Risk
Status: Published (informal)

Purpose and scope
-----------------
This Information Security Statement aims to provide an overarching description of NeoTrust Ambiguous’s posture regarding information protection, risk governance and operational security. It is intentionally non-prescriptive in certain sections to allow flexibility across product lines and contractual tiers. The statement applies broadly to NeoTrust corporate operations, managed services, and customer-facing platforms, subject to specific contractual variations and exceptions defined in client agreements.

Philosophy & approach
---------------------
NeoTrust takes a pragmatic, risk-aware approach to security implementation. The company prefers solutions that balance operational agility and risk mitigation, and therefore some of its controls are applied selectively based on perceived impact and the customer’s chosen service tier. Where appropriate, NeoTrust references established frameworks (for example ISO/IEC 27001, SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria, and cloud provider security guidelines) and aligns internal practice to the extent that it is feasible and justifiable from a cost, performance and integration perspective.

Governance and responsibilities
-------------------------------
The security governance model is layered and adaptive. The Head of Security & Risk is accountable for policy oversight and high-level coordination, but day-to-day security decisions are often delegated to product security owners and engineering leads. A periodic governance forum convenes stakeholders across product, operations, legal and sales to review major risks and open remediation actions. The cadence of governance reviews may vary — commonly quarterly, but occasionally on an as-needed basis for critical issues.

Risk assessment and acceptance
------------------------------
NeoTrust maintains a risk register and conducts product-line-specific risk assessments. The treatment of identified risks follows a standard triage: remediation, acceptance, transfer (insurance/contractual), or mitigation via compensating controls. For certain low-impact or legacy components, the company may elect to accept residual risk subject to documented rationale and periodic re-evaluation. Risk thresholds are communicated to senior management and reflected in engagement-specific statements of work when needed.

Control implementation and evidence
-----------------------------------
Controls are implemented across technical, organizational and procedural layers. Technical controls include perimeter and application firewalls, WAFs, basic IDS/IPS configurations, and cloud-native security features where available. Organizational controls include vendor reviews and contractual DPAs. In many cases, evidence of control efficacy (logs, test reports, certificates) is available on request; however, availability may be contingent on the client’s contract level or NDAs. NeoTrust will generally provide documentary or demonstrable evidence to customers after appropriate diligence and contractual terms are established.

Transparency and limitations
----------------------------
NeoTrust endeavors to be transparent about its security posture; nevertheless, certain disclosures cannot be applied uniformly. For operational security reasons, the company may withhold specific technical details publicly but will discuss them with clients under NDA. The words “may”, “typically”, and “where applicable” recur in this statement by design — they signal that specific implementations are context-dependent and that contractual terms take precedence.

Review and continuous improvement
---------------------------------
This Statement is reviewed periodically; emergent threats, significant incidents, or strategic shifts may accelerate reviews. Stakeholders are invited to submit clarifying questions; responses will be routed to the Head of Security & Risk and, where necessary, to legal for contractual clarification.

=== DOCUMENT 2 : Access & Identity Expectations (Extended) ===
Version: 1.5 – Oct 2025
Language: English
Owner: Identity & Access Lead
Status: Guidance (operational)

Overview and intent
-------------------
This document frames NeoTrust’s expectations and current practices concerning identity, authentication and authorization across managed services and tenant models. The objective is to set realistic customer expectations while documenting the practical constraints that sometimes influence implementation choices.

Identity models and provisioning
--------------------------------
NeoTrust supports several identity models: full SSO with federated identity providers (IdP) for enterprise customers, hybrid SSO/local account combinations for smaller clients, and temporary credentials for managed-service interventions. Where SSO is supported, the IdP configuration is typically handled in collaboration with the customer; for customers without an IdP, NeoTrust may deploy internal accounts with scoped privileges.

Account lifecycle and reviews
-----------------------------
Account creation, modification and termination follow an operational workflow; however, the degree of automation varies. Large enterprise deployments often integrate with customer HR/IdP via SCIM or similar provisioning; for other customers, account lifecycle events are processed manually by operations staff. Access reviews are performed — cadence varies: some tenants receive quarterly reviews, others semi-annually or on demand. NeoTrust may propose quarterly cadence as a best practice; actual cadence is frequently aligned to client contractual terms.

Authentication and MFA
----------------------
NeoTrust enforces multi-factor authentication for privileged user classes and administrative console access in most environments. For certain legacy integrations and development tooling, MFA may be relaxed to accommodate operational constraints; compensating controls (restricted network access, short-lived credentials) are implemented where feasible. NeoTrust recommends MFA across the board, and offers guidance and assistance to customers to enable it in their environments.

Authorization and least privilege
--------------------------------
Role-based access control (RBAC) and least-privilege principles are the default model. However, for speed of operations and in emergency scenarios, temporary elevated access (break-glass) mechanisms are provided to authorized operators, accompanied by logging and retrospective review. The processes around break-glass can vary slightly from one deployment to another; NeoTrust documents each event and may request post-event approval from the customer.

Privileged account monitoring
-----------------------------
Privileged activities are logged and retained according to tenant-specific retention policies. NeoTrust aims to capture essential telemetry (who, what, when, where); however, the level of detail and retention period may differ by subscription tier. Customers may opt for extended logging and SIEM integration as an add-on.

Exceptions and contractual precedence
------------------------------------
Operational exceptions are documented and subject to approval by NeoTrust’s security governance. Contractual terms governing a specific customer supersede this guidance; in all cases, NeoTrust will seek to negotiate pragmatic solutions consistent with both security posture and business needs.

=== DOCUMENT 3 : Guide des mots de passe et identifiants (Extended) ===
Version : 1.1 – Octobre 2025
Langue : Français
Propriétaire : Responsable Sécurité Opérationnelle
Statut : Recommandation

Introduction
------------
Ce document détaille les recommandations de NeoTrust en matière de création, stockage, rotation et partage des mots de passe et autres secrets. Il vise à concilier sécurité opérationnelle et contraintes d’intégration avec des outils tiers ou des processus existants chez nos clients.

Bonnes pratiques recommandées
-----------------------------
- **Phrases de passe** : privilégier les phrases de passe d’au moins 16 caractères pour les comptes humains. Lorsque l’usage d’un gestionnaire de mots de passe est possible, il est fortement recommandé de l’adopter.
- **Gestion des secrets** : les secrets d’application et clés API doivent, dans la mesure du possible, être gérés via une solution de type KMS/KeyVault. NeoTrust recommande l’usage de solutions managées (AWS KMS, Azure Key Vault, HashiCorp Vault) selon la plateforme hôte.
- **Rotation** : la rotation régulière des secrets est encouragée ; la fréquence peut varier (p.ex. 90–365 jours) en fonction de la criticité. Pour certains comptes machine à haut volume, la rotation peut être automatisée et pilotée par des jobs orchestrés.
- **Stockage partagé** : l’usage de coffres partagés est toléré pour les opérations mais doit être strictement limité aux personnels habilités et faire l’objet d’un suivi d’accès.

Contraintes et exceptions
-------------------------
Il existe des cas pratiques où des outils ou intégrations imposent des formats ou des contraintes : certains systèmes CI/CD, appliances réseau anciennes, ou intégrations SaaS tierces peuvent nécessiter l’emploi d’un secret long immuable. Dans ces contextes, NeoTrust peut mettre en place des mesures compensatoires (réstriction d’exposition réseau, rotation programmée via wrapper) et documenter le risque.

Gestion des incidents liés aux secrets
-------------------------------------
Toute suspicion de compromission d’un secret doit être remontée immédiatement via le canal désigné. Les actions habituelles sont : révocation immédiate, génération d’un secret neuf, et investigation court terme. NeoTrust recommande la révocation rapide des secrets déployés dans des environnements partagés.

Sensibilisation et formation
----------------------------
Un module de sensibilisation traite du phishing et du bon usage des coffres ; ce dernier est dispensé lors des onboarding pour les profils techniques. Toutefois, la fréquence des sessions et la couverture varient selon les lignes produit et les clients.

=== DOCUMENT 4 : Data Handling — High Level Considerations (Extended) ===
Version: 1.2 – Oct 2025
Language: English
Owner: Data Protection & Compliance Lead

Overview and contractual nuance
-------------------------------
NeoTrust’s approach to data handling is deliberately customer-needs oriented; that is, the company defines baseline practices while allowing contractual deviations for data residency, retention and export controls. This document outlines typical practices and highlights decision points likely to be covered in contractual annexes.

Data classification and labeling
--------------------------------
NeoTrust encourages customers to adopt a classification taxonomy and will support labeling at dataset level where integrated tooling permits. Typical categories include Public, Internal, Confidential and Restricted. Classification may be performed by customers or jointly with NeoTrust during onboarding for managed services.

Encryption and key management
-----------------------------
At minimum, network transport uses TLS 1.2+ (TLS 1.3 where supported). Data-at-rest encryption is provided by the underlying cloud platform; NeoTrust recommends provider-managed KMS for standard tiers and tenant-dedicated KMS for higher trust tiers. Key lifecycle management is usually conducted by the cloud provider; NeoTrust offers optional managed key services where client needs demand dedicated control.

Backups, retention and immutability
----------------------------------
Backups of core services are produced with frequencies reflecting product SLAs. Retention policies vary: typical values are 30 days (standard), 90 days (business), 365+ days (archival/premium). Immutable backups are offered as a premium capability for certain services; customers concerned by regulatory or investigational needs are encouraged to opt for these plans.

Data subject rights and deletion
-------------------------------
NeoTrust implements processes enabling data subject requests (access, deletion, portability). The degree of automation depends on the data architecture and on whether data is stored in shared multi-tenant systems or dedicated tenants. Deletion workflows may involve manual verification steps for complex datasets.

Cross-border transfers and legal compliance
------------------------------------------
Cross-border transfers proceed under contractual safeguards (SCCs, adequacy decisions) where applicable. For some geographies, NeoTrust may restrict export out of region unless express contractual permission exists.

Operational caveats and evidence
-------------------------------
NeoTrust can provide procedural documentation and evidence for many controls, but some evidence types (e.g., raw logs, internal change records) are only disclosed under audit terms or via secure channels agreed in contract. For clients requiring stronger evidential chains, NeoTrust recommends premium engagements with expanded logging retention and independent attestations.

=== DOCUMENT 5 : Sauvegarde et Rétention — Notes étendues (Extended) ===
Version : 1.1 – Octobre 2025
Langue : Français
Propriétaire : Responsable Infrastructure
Statut : Politique opérationnelle (niveau guidance)

Contexte et principes
---------------------
NeoTrust opère plusieurs offres et chaque offre peut embarquer un schéma de sauvegarde différent. Les paragraphes ci-dessous présentent les pratiques les plus courantes et les options disponibles, en soulignant que les engagements chiffrés doivent être contractualisés.

Fréquences et méthodes
----------------------
- Sauvegardes différentielles et complètes sont effectuées selon la nature du service : journalières pour les bases critiques, hebdomadaires pour certains systèmes de reporting, ponctuelles pour les environnements de développement.
- Les sauvegardes sont stockées sur des supports cloud redondants, avec possibilités d’export vers un stockage “cold” pour archival.
- La vérification d’intégrité est effectuée de manière périodique ; la fréquence et l’automatisation de ces vérifications peuvent varier selon le plan choisi.

Rétention et immuabilité
------------------------
- Les durées de rétention usuelles indiquées en documentation commerciale sont indicatives (30/90/365). La mise en place d’une immuabilité effective (WORM) est proposée en option sur certains services : l’activation est alors soumise à configuration préalable et à disponibilité régionale.
- Les délais contractuels pour la suppression ou l’export des sauvegardes sont définis dans la DPA : NeoTrust s’engage à coopérer pour l’extraction ou la purge sous conditions raisonnables.

Procédure de restauration
-------------------------
- Les restaurations sont principalement orchestrées par l’équipe support ou par les ingénieurs en charge du service. Le délai et la granularité (fichier, base, volume complet) dépendent de la topologie et des droits d’accès.
- Les scénarios de tests de restauration sont conduits périodiquement pour certaines offres ; sur d’autres, ils sont réalisés à la demande ou après incidents significatifs.

Logs, traçabilité et preuve
---------------------------
- Les journaux d’activité liés aux opérations de sauvegarde (création, suppression, restauration) sont parfois archivés et traités comme logs d’audit. La conservation et la divulgation de ces logs sont soumises à la politique de rétention choisie par le client.

Risques connus et mesures compensatoires
---------------------------------------
- Lorsque l’immuabilité n’est pas activée, NeoTrust recommande des mesures compensatoires (chiffrement de sauvegarde, contrôles d’accès renforcés, revue d’intégrité).  
- Les scénarios légaux ou investigatifs peuvent nécessiter des exportations manuelles qui impliquent des délais opérationnels.

Révision et amélioration continue
---------------------------------
Ce document est maintenu par l’équipe Infrastructure ; les évolutions technologiques (nouveaux fournisseurs de stockage, capacités immutables locales) sont évaluées et peuvent être proposées comme options commerciales.

=== DOCUMENT 6 : Network & Perimeter Security Position Paper (Extended) ===
Version : 1.1 – Oct 2025
Language : English
Owner : Head of Infrastructure
Status : Reference guidance

Purpose and audience
--------------------
This paper describes NeoTrust’s philosophy regarding network and perimeter security. It aims to align technical implementation with flexible operational realities, particularly where infrastructure is shared between customer environments or hosted under different compliance contexts.

Network design principles
-------------------------
NeoTrust adopts a layered network model built around the principle of “defense in depth”.  
Each environment is segmented logically — physical segmentation may be provided where feasible.  
The implementation of micro-segmentation is based on the maturity of the tenant: some clients leverage dedicated VPCs or VNets, others operate within shared logical segments secured by routing policies.

Firewall and traffic control
----------------------------
Inbound and outbound traffic is controlled using a combination of cloud-native firewalls, security groups, and access control lists.  
Rule sets are reviewed periodically — the cadence depends on service criticality and volume of change.  
For certain lower-tier offerings, automatic rule reviews may not be enforced; instead, event-driven reviews occur when incidents or alerts arise.

Perimeter defense and WAF
-------------------------
NeoTrust deploys web application firewalls (WAFs) for its main SaaS endpoints.  
Configuration templates are standardized but can be overridden to match customer application patterns.  
False positive management and tuning are handled jointly by operations and development teams; frequency of tuning depends on traffic volume and anomaly reports.

Monitoring and detection
------------------------
Network monitoring combines automated tools (VPC flow logs, IDS alerts) with manual periodic reviews.  
Some telemetry streams are aggregated into a central log environment; others remain local to reduce latency or cost.  
The detection coverage is broad but not exhaustive — certain internal microservices may not be monitored continuously due to data volume constraints.

Limitations and compensations
-----------------------------
Legacy systems hosted in hybrid environments may still rely on static IP-based allowlists instead of zero-trust models.  
Where this occurs, compensating controls include MFA enforcement and restricted SSH access.  
NeoTrust continues to evolve towards fully software-defined perimeters.

=== DOCUMENT 7 : Gestion des vulnérabilités et correctifs (Étendu) ===
Version : 1.2 – Octobre 2025
Langue : Français
Propriétaire : Responsable Sécurité Technique
Statut : Procédure cadre

Objectif et portée
------------------
Ce document présente la stratégie et les processus associés à la détection, l’évaluation et la correction des vulnérabilités au sein de NeoTrust.  
L’approche repose sur la priorisation par criticité et la flexibilité selon les environnements.

Identification et suivi
-----------------------
Les vulnérabilités sont identifiées par différents canaux :
- Scans automatiques internes (hebdomadaires ou mensuels selon le périmètre) ;
- Bulletins de sécurité éditeurs (Microsoft, AWS, Linux distributions) ;
- Alertes issues de la communauté ou des CERT.

Les résultats sont centralisés dans un tableau de suivi ; le niveau de détail et la fréquence de mise à jour varient selon les équipes.

Évaluation et priorisation
--------------------------
NeoTrust applique une échelle inspirée du CVSS pour évaluer les vulnérabilités.  
Les correctifs sont planifiés selon la sévérité, mais les délais cibles (SLA internes) sont variables :  
- Critique : correction rapide (objectif de 7 jours ouvrés, sans garantie)  
- Haute : 30 jours  
- Moyenne : selon disponibilité  
Les exceptions doivent être justifiées et validées par le responsable technique.

Correction et déploiement
-------------------------
Les correctifs peuvent être appliqués via pipeline CI/CD ou manuellement, selon la maturité du projet.  
Les environnements legacy ne bénéficient pas toujours d’automatisation ; la mise à jour est alors “best effort”.  
Des mesures compensatoires sont acceptables (blocage d’IP, filtrage applicatif, désactivation de fonctionnalités).

Tests et validation
-------------------
Après correction, des tests de non-régression sont exécutés, bien qu’ils ne soient pas systématiques.  
Les scans de vérification post-patch sont effectués sur échantillon.

Communication et reporting
---------------------------
Un rapport de vulnérabilité consolidé est produit une à deux fois par an et présenté à la direction technique.  
Des résumés sont fournis aux clients qui en font la demande.

=== DOCUMENT 8 : Incident Response Plan — Operational Overview (Extended) ===
Version : 2.0 – Oct 2025
Language : English
Owner : Incident Coordinator
Status : Live (process documentation)

Purpose
-------
This document summarizes NeoTrust’s approach to detecting, reporting, and managing security incidents.  
The plan focuses on flexibility, recognizing that incident specifics and customer contexts often dictate response nuance.

Detection and reporting
-----------------------
Incidents are primarily detected through a mix of:
- automated alerting (monitoring, IDS, CSPM)
- customer-reported anomalies
- internal staff observations

When an incident is suspected, it is logged in the internal tracker and a triage team is convened.  
The size and composition of the triage team depend on the incident scope.

Containment and eradication
---------------------------
Immediate containment steps include isolation of affected instances, credential rotation, and disabling external integrations where applicable.  
In cloud environments, temporary firewall rules or instance terminations may be used to halt spread.

Root cause and forensics
------------------------
NeoTrust maintains limited in-house forensic capabilities; deeper analyses may be outsourced to a partner under NDA.  
Root cause analysis (RCA) is performed for high-severity events; medium and low-severity events are reviewed only if repeated or customer-impacting.

Communication and disclosure
----------------------------
Incident notifications follow contractual obligations.  
Customers may be notified within a target of 48 hours “where relevant” — actual timing depends on incident classification and data involvement.  
Public disclosure is coordinated by legal and communications if deemed necessary.

Lessons learned and improvement
-------------------------------
Each major incident is reviewed post-resolution, with outcomes recorded in an internal Confluence space.  
Actions may lead to process updates, tooling improvements, or retraining.

=== DOCUMENT 10 : Cryptography & Key Management Policy (Extended) ===
Version : 1.3 – Oct 2025
Language : English
Owner : Security Architecture Lead
Status : Policy (in force)

Purpose
-------
This policy defines the cryptographic controls and key management practices applicable across NeoTrust systems.  
It establishes a flexible baseline that may be adjusted per product line or contractual engagement.

Scope
-----
Applies to data encryption (in transit and at rest), digital signing, and key lifecycle management.  
Includes SaaS services, internal systems, and development tooling where feasible.

Encryption principles
---------------------
- Transport: TLS 1.2 minimum; TLS 1.3 preferred where supported.  
- Data at rest: provider-managed encryption (AES-256 or equivalent) is default.  
- End-to-end encryption (E2EE) is available for select workloads but not universally applied.

Key management
--------------
- Keys are typically managed by cloud-native KMS services (AWS, Azure, GCP).  
- Key rotation is enforced at least annually or upon administrative change.  
- Certain lower-tier systems may use shared keys; these are rotated manually.

Algorithm standards and migration
---------------------------------
NeoTrust follows recognized standards (AES, RSA, ECDSA) but allows transitional use of deprecated algorithms for backward compatibility with third-party integrations.  
Migration roadmaps exist but are staged to minimize disruption.

Evidence and documentation
--------------------------
Cryptographic architecture diagrams are available under NDA.  
Customers may request evidence of encryption configuration, but some details (key identifiers, internal HSM policies) are redacted for security.

Limitations
-----------
NeoTrust does not currently enforce hardware-backed key storage for all environments.  
Work is ongoing to evaluate HSM-backed KMS expansion in 2026.

=== DOCUMENT 11 : Politique de gestion du changement (Étendue) ===
Version : 1.2 – Octobre 2025  
Langue : Français  
Propriétaire : Directeur Technique  
Statut : Procédure cadre  

Objectif  
---------
Établir un cadre souple permettant de contrôler, documenter et valider les changements affectant les environnements de production et de préproduction, sans alourdir les cycles de déploiement.

Portée  
-------
Inclut : mises à jour logicielles, configurations réseau, paramètres de sécurité, changements d’infrastructure.  
Exclut : décisions organisationnelles et changements contractuels clients.

Processus de demande de changement  
----------------------------------
Les changements sont proposés via :  
- un ticket interne ;  
- une pull request ;  
- ou, en cas d’urgence, un message Slack accompagné d’un résumé technique.  

L’approbation dépend du niveau de risque :  
- mineur → validation d’un pair ;  
- majeur → avis du directeur technique.  

Les changements critiques peuvent être exécutés immédiatement si le risque de downtime dépasse celui du déploiement.

Tests et validations  
---------------------
Les validations s’effectuent en staging, sauf pour les corrections urgentes.  
Des environnements de test isolés existent mais ne couvrent pas tous les cas ; les tests unitaires et d’intégration ne sont pas toujours automatisés.

Mise en production  
-------------------
Les déploiements passent par CI/CD ; les scripts contiennent des étapes de rollback manuel.  
En cas d’échec, la restauration d’un build précédent est privilégiée.  

Documentation et journalisation  
-------------------------------
Chaque changement doit être consigné dans Git ; cependant, la granularité des messages varie.  
Un changelog global est généré trimestriellement pour les besoins d’audit.

Amélioration continue  
----------------------
Une réunion de revue post-changement est organisée de manière informelle pour les incidents récurrents.  
Un tableau de suivi des déploiements est prévu pour 2026.

=== DOCUMENT 12 : Secure Development & SDLC Guidelines (Extended) ===
Version : 1.1 – Oct 2025  
Language : English  
Owner : Head of Engineering Quality  

Purpose  
--------
To formalize NeoTrust’s expectations and practices around secure software development, while keeping flexibility for agile teams and legacy modules.

Development lifecycle  
---------------------
NeoTrust’s software development lifecycle (SDLC) loosely follows agile methodologies. Each product team defines its own sprint cadence and documentation rigor.  
A global set of guidelines exists but is not enforced at code-merge level.

Security checkpoints  
--------------------
- Code reviews are “expected” for all merges but may be bypassed under time pressure.  
- Static analysis (SAST) runs automatically on main repositories; smaller modules are analyzed on demand.  
- Dynamic scanning (DAST) is integrated for flagship applications, less so for internal tools.  
- Dependency management uses automated vulnerability scanning, yet remediations depend on release priorities.

Threat modeling and design  
---------------------------
NeoTrust encourages early threat modeling for new features. In practice, only strategic projects receive formal sessions. Documentation of mitigations is optional.  
Architecture diagrams are maintained when significant structural changes occur but not version-controlled systematically.

Security testing and CI/CD  
--------------------------
Security tests form part of the CI pipeline “where feasible”. Developers can skip these checks for low-risk features by flagging exceptions.  
Build break thresholds may vary per repository; some legacy repos have disabled the enforcement mechanism entirely.

Governance and evidence  
-----------------------
The Security Engineering Lead consolidates quarterly reports summarizing vulnerabilities fixed and open. Evidence of secure coding activities can be provided under audit, albeit with caveats due to partial coverage.

=== DOCUMENT 13 : Journalisation et supervision (Étendu) ===
Version : 1.1 – Octobre 2025  
Langue : Français  
Propriétaire : Responsable SOC  

Objectif  
---------
Assurer la traçabilité minimale des activités critiques et la détection d’événements de sécurité significatifs.

Collecte et sources  
-------------------
Les journaux système et applicatifs sont collectés via des agents légers.  
Certaines applications héritées publient leurs logs en syslog ; d’autres écrivent localement avant agrégation différée.  
L’exhaustivité n’est pas garantie ; la couverture dépend de la priorité du service.

Corrélation et détection  
------------------------
Les règles de corrélation sont maintenues dans Elastic SIEM ; elles sont revues “selon disponibilité”.  
Les alertes de faible criticité peuvent être ignorées automatiquement pour éviter le bruit.  
Aucune règle n’est dédiée à la détection d’abus de privilèges ; ce cas est couvert indirectement par les logs d’accès.

Rétention et accès  
------------------

=== DOCUMENT 21 : Cloud & Multitenant Isolation Guidelines (Extended) ===
Version : 1.3 – Oct 2025  
Language : English  
Owner : Cloud Architecture Team  

Purpose  
-------
To describe NeoTrust’s principles for secure isolation within its multitenant SaaS environment and customer-dedicated deployments.

Architecture principles  
-----------------------
NeoTrust operates on a hybrid cloud model. Each tenant resides logically within a distinct namespace or account boundary.  
While physical separation is rare, resource tagging and access controls provide effective isolation “in most cases”.

Control mechanisms  
------------------
- **Authentication and authorization:** implemented via IAM roles scoped per tenant. Some shared microservices may temporarily hold cross-tenant privileges for diagnostic purposes.  
- **Data segregation:** handled through database schema separation. Encryption keys are tenant-specific for premium tiers, but shared at storage level for standard tiers.  
- **Network segmentation:** enforced by virtual private networks and route filters; exceptions may occur for third-party integrations.

Monitoring and detection  
------------------------
Tenant-level logging enables anomaly tracking. However, metrics aggregation may obscure per-tenant patterns.  
Alerts triggered by one tenant’s event stream could affect others if misclassified — mitigations are being refined.

Residual risks  
--------------
Shared infrastructure introduces residual risks of side-channel exposure and resource contention.  
Customers are informed through the Security Exhibit that “logical separation is applied as industry best practice permits”.

Future improvements  
-------------------
A transition to full per-tenant encryption contexts and service mesh enforcement is planned for 2026.
