=== DOCUMENT 1 : Security Policy ===
Version : 1.1 – Reviewed March 2025  
Owner : CTO / Security Lead  
Status : Draft in continuous improvement  

1. Purpose  
-----------
The purpose of this Security Policy is to provide general guidance on how CloudFirst manages the protection of its systems, customer data, and operations. The company acknowledges that security is a shared responsibility across all employees and contractors. While no formal ISO or SOC certification is currently held, CloudFirst aims to progressively align with recognized best practices.

2. Scope  
---------
This policy applies to all employees, interns, and external service providers who have access to CloudFirst systems and infrastructure. It covers SaaS environments hosted on AWS and Google Cloud, as well as the company’s internal tools (GitHub, Slack, Google Workspace).

3. Security Governance  
----------------------
CloudFirst has designated a “Security Lead” within the DevOps team to coordinate daily security matters.  
A monthly review is held informally between the CTO, the Security Lead, and the Engineering Manager to discuss vulnerabilities, incidents, and priorities.  
There is no official risk committee, but critical issues are escalated during engineering stand-up meetings.

4. Key Security Principles  
---------------------------
- **Least Privilege:** Users are expected to have only the access necessary for their role, although exceptions exist for some senior developers.
- **Shared Responsibility:** Security is considered everyone’s concern, though not all employees are formally trained.
- **Continuous Improvement:** Policies are revised “as needed,” often after an issue arises.

5. Information Handling  
------------------------
Confidential customer data is stored on cloud databases with default encryption provided by the platform.  
Access to production data is restricted to a few engineers, but no formal audit trail exists for all operations.  
Backups are automated, though their integrity is not regularly verified.

6. Monitoring and Reporting  
----------------------------
A Slack channel named `#security` is used to report suspicious events. Employees are encouraged to post there, but no ticketing workflow ensures proper follow-up.  
Some AWS GuardDuty alerts are received by email but not centrally correlated.

7. Policy Review  
----------------
This document is reviewed annually or after any major incident. Current review responsibility lies with the CTO, who can delegate revisions as needed.

=== DOCUMENT 2 : Access Control Policy ===
Version : 1.0 – January 2025  
Owner : Security Lead  

1. Objective  
-------------
This Access Control Policy describes how user accounts and permissions are managed within CloudFirst systems. The goal is to ensure reasonable protection against unauthorized access while keeping productivity high.

2. Roles and Responsibilities  
-------------------------------
- The **Security Lead** manages production access.  
- The **Engineering Manager** handles development-level access.  
- HR creates and disables accounts in Google Workspace upon hiring or termination.  
Formal approval workflows are not yet implemented; requests are validated through Slack or verbal confirmation.

3. Authentication  
------------------
Most corporate systems rely on Google Workspace single sign-on (SSO). MFA is enforced for administrative accounts but not for all users, due to compatibility issues with some CI/CD tools.  
Passwords must be at least eight characters, but there is no complexity enforcement on every third-party service.  

4. Authorization  
-----------------
Access to AWS and GitHub is granted via group-based roles. However, the documentation of group rights is incomplete.  
Some shared credentials still exist for internal services and are kept in a shared vault managed by 1Password, without full audit logging enabled.

5. Account Review  
------------------
Access reviews are performed occasionally when someone leaves or when a new client onboarding requires adjustments. There is no formal quarterly review process.

6. Privileged Access  
---------------------
Admin credentials are stored in 1Password. The root AWS credentials are known by both co-founders.  
No Just-In-Time (JIT) or Privileged Access Management (PAM) solution is deployed at this stage.

7. Policy Update  
-----------------
The policy is expected to evolve as the company matures. A review is planned for Q2 2026, ideally to align with ISO 27001 Annex A.9 requirements.

=== DOCUMENT 3 : Data Protection & Backup Policy ===
Version : 1.2 – February 2025  
Owner : Head of Engineering  
Language : French  

1. Objet  
---------
La présente politique vise à encadrer la protection, la sauvegarde et la restauration des données traitées par CloudFirst, incluant les environnements de production et les espaces internes.  
Les principes décrits ici s’appliquent à toutes les données clients et internes, sauf mention contraire.

2. Classification des données  
------------------------------
Les données sont classées de manière simple :  
- **Confidentielles :** données clients, credentials, secrets d’API  
- **Internes :** documentation technique, code source  
- **Publiques :** pages marketing, communications externes  

Aucune grille de sensibilité n’est encore formalisée. Le chiffrement est appliqué “par défaut” sur AWS (S3 AES-256), mais pas documenté.

3. Sauvegarde  
--------------
Les sauvegardes sont effectuées quotidiennement via les scripts de déploiement Terraform.  
Elles sont stockées dans un bucket S3 séparé avec une rétention de 14 jours.  
Aucune stratégie d’immuabilité (“immutable storage”) n’est encore mise en place.  
Le test de restauration est fait “à la demande”, généralement après un incident.

4. Restauration  
----------------
En cas d’incident, le responsable technique exécute manuellement la procédure de restauration.  
Aucun RTO/RPO n’est défini formellement, mais l’objectif implicite est de restaurer le service sous 24 h ouvrées.

5. Confidentialité et chiffrement  
----------------------------------
Les sauvegardes sont chiffrées via AWS-KMS.  
Les clés sont gérées automatiquement par la plateforme cloud.  
Aucune rotation manuelle n’est prévue.

6. Revue et amélioration  
--------------------------
Une refonte de la stratégie de sauvegarde est prévue courant 2026, avec l’objectif d’introduire la vérification automatique de l’intégrité et la conservation hors ligne.

=== DOCUMENT 4 : Incident Management Process ===
Version : 1.0 – December 2024  
Owner : CTO / Engineering Manager  
Language : English  

1. Purpose  
-----------
This document defines how CloudFirst handles security incidents and system disruptions.  
The intent is to provide a basic framework to detect, report, and resolve issues that could affect service availability, confidentiality, or integrity.

2. Scope  
---------
Applies to all production and staging environments hosted in AWS and to key third-party SaaS services (GitHub, Slack, Google Workspace, and Stripe).  
It also includes internal laptops when they are used to deploy code or manage infrastructure.

3. Roles and Responsibilities  
------------------------------
- **CTO:** ultimate decision-maker during a major incident.  
- **Engineering Manager:** coordinates the technical response.  
- **Developers:** report anomalies and assist in debugging.  
- **Support team:** communicates with customers.  

There is currently no formal “incident response team” or “on-call rotation.”  
When an alert arises, engineers self-organize to address it.

4. Detection and Reporting  
---------------------------
Incidents are mainly detected through user complaints or platform alerts (AWS CloudWatch, GitHub Security tab).  
Some alerts are ignored due to false positives, as no dedicated triage system exists.  
When an incident seems real, engineers post a message in the Slack channel `#urgent-tech` and tag the CTO.  

5. Investigation and Containment  
---------------------------------
Containment typically involves redeploying services or rotating keys manually.  
For major issues, the engineering team uses a shared Google Doc to track actions taken.  
There is no centralized incident management system (e.g., Jira or PagerDuty integration).

6. Communication  
-----------------
Customer communication is handled ad hoc via email or Slack Connect.  
The company doesn’t maintain a status page yet, but an update mechanism is under consideration.

7. Post-Incident Review  
------------------------
After resolution, the CTO summarizes lessons learned in a private Notion page.  
Some incidents result in technical improvements, but formal RCA (Root Cause Analysis) documents are not mandatory.  

8. Review  
----------
This process will be reviewed every 12 months or following a major production issue.

=== DOCUMENT 5 : Password Guidelines ===
Version : 1.0 – January 2025  
Owner : Security Lead  
Language : French  

1. Objet  
---------
Ce document décrit les bonnes pratiques de gestion des mots de passe pour les employés, partenaires et prestataires ayant accès aux systèmes CloudFirst.  
L’objectif est de réduire les risques d’accès non autorisés et d’assurer un niveau de protection minimal.

2. Règles de création  
----------------------
Les utilisateurs doivent définir des mots de passe comportant au moins 8 caractères, incluant une majuscule et un chiffre.  
Il est recommandé, mais non obligatoire, d’utiliser des caractères spéciaux.  
Certains outils externes (par exemple, certains scripts CI) ne supportent pas ces contraintes, et des exceptions sont tolérées.

3. Réutilisation et changement  
-------------------------------
Le changement de mot de passe est demandé à la création d’un compte, puis à la discrétion de l’utilisateur.  
Il n’y a pas de politique de rotation imposée ni d’alerte automatique.  
En cas d’incident de sécurité connu, les utilisateurs sont invités à réinitialiser manuellement leur mot de passe.

4. Stockage et partage  
-----------------------
Les mots de passe critiques (root, comptes d’administration) sont stockés dans un coffre 1Password partagé entre les fondateurs et quelques ingénieurs.  
Le partage par Slack est proscrit, mais a déjà été observé à plusieurs reprises.  
Il n’y a pas de surveillance automatique de ces pratiques.

5. Authentification multifacteur (MFA)  
---------------------------------------
L’activation MFA est obligatoire pour les comptes administrateurs AWS, mais optionnelle pour les autres utilisateurs.  
Une migration vers l’authentification forte est envisagée, sans calendrier défini.

6. Revue et amélioration  
--------------------------
Un audit interne des pratiques de mot de passe est prévu pour 2026, dans la perspective d’une certification SOC 2.  
Les futures versions incluront des recommandations sur les tokens et clés API.

=== DOCUMENT 6 : Change Management Policy ===
Version : 1.1 – March 2025  
Owner : Engineering Manager  
Language : English  

1. Purpose  
-----------
This document defines how CloudFirst manages technical and configuration changes in its production and staging environments.  
Although no formal CAB (Change Advisory Board) exists, the goal is to ensure minimal disruption to customer services.

2. Scope  
---------
Covers infrastructure updates, code deployments, network configuration changes, and system-level patches.  
Does not include HR or organizational changes.

3. Change Request  
------------------
Developers announce changes via Slack or GitHub pull requests.  
Approval is given by a peer or the Engineering Manager, depending on impact.  
Emergency fixes (“hot patches”) are deployed directly with post-facto documentation.

4. Testing and Validation  
--------------------------
Each change must be validated in staging before production deployment.  
However, some smaller updates (configuration flags, monitoring rules) are directly applied to production when time is limited.  

5. Rollback  
------------
Rollback is manual and not always tested.  
In some cases, redeploying a previous version from CI/CD artifacts is the only rollback procedure.

6. Documentation  
-----------------
Changes are described in Git commit messages and occasionally summarized in Notion.  
No central log of changes exists yet.

7. Review  
----------
There is no periodic audit of the change process.  
Future improvements include introducing a lightweight CAB and an automatic change log within CI/CD pipelines.

=== DOCUMENT 7 : Remote Access Procedure ===
Version : 1.0 – April 2025  
Owner : CTO  
Language : French  

1. Objet  
---------
Ce document décrit les règles de connexion à distance aux environnements techniques de CloudFirst.  
Il vise à encadrer l’usage du VPN, des connexions SSH et des outils d’administration externes afin de limiter les risques d’accès non autorisés.

2. Portée  
----------
Applicable à tous les employés et prestataires disposant d’un accès aux environnements AWS, GitHub, et à l’intranet interne.  
Les postes personnels utilisés dans le cadre du télétravail sont inclus.

3. VPN et chiffrement  
----------------------
Les connexions distantes s’effectuent via un VPN OpenVPN hébergé sur AWS EC2.  
Le chiffrement TLS est activé, mais les certificats ne sont pas renouvelés automatiquement.  
Certains collaborateurs utilisent encore des connexions SSH directes lorsqu’ils rencontrent des problèmes de compatibilité avec le VPN.  
L’usage du split-tunneling n’est pas interdit, bien qu’il présente des risques connus.

4. Authentification  
--------------------
L’accès VPN repose sur un couple login/mot de passe et un certificat client.  
L’authentification multifacteur est prévue pour 2026.  
Les identifiants sont créés manuellement par l’équipe DevOps et désactivés à la demande du manager.

5. Sécurité des postes  
-----------------------
Aucune politique centralisée de chiffrement des disques ou de verrouillage automatique n’est imposée.  
Les collaborateurs sont encouragés à utiliser les outils de sécurité natifs de leur OS (BitLocker, FileVault).  
Aucun contrôle de conformité n’est effectué avant connexion.

6. Surveillance  
---------------
Les connexions VPN sont journalisées, mais les logs ne sont analysés qu’en cas d’incident.  
Un rapport mensuel d’accès distant est prévu mais rarement produit.

7. Revue  
---------
Le présent document sera révisé à l’occasion de la migration vers un nouveau fournisseur VPN.

=== DOCUMENT 8 : Business Continuity Summary ===
Version : 0.9 – December 2024  
Owner : CTO  
Language : English  

1. Objective  
-------------
This Business Continuity Summary outlines the main principles guiding CloudFirst’s preparedness for service disruptions.  
It is not a formal Business Continuity Plan (BCP) but provides minimal reference points for recovery expectations.

2. Scope  
---------
Covers production systems hosted on AWS and Google Cloud, CI/CD pipelines, and critical SaaS services (GitHub, Slack, Google Workspace).  
Office operations and HR systems are not yet included.

3. Strategy  
------------
The company’s continuity strategy relies on cloud resilience and rapid redeployment rather than on-premise redundancy.  
No separate DR (Disaster Recovery) environment is maintained.  
Snapshots of key services are taken daily, but there is no automatic failover.

4. Roles  
---------
During major incidents, the CTO coordinates response efforts.  
Engineering leads handle technical recovery, and the support team manages customer communication.  
No formal alternates are designated.

5. RTO / RPO  
--------------
No quantified Recovery Time Objective (RTO) or Recovery Point Objective (RPO) has been defined.  
The internal target is “service back within 24 hours”.

6. Testing  
-----------
Restoration tests have been done twice in 2024.  
Results were positive but not documented in detail.

7. Review and Improvements  
----------------------------
A full BCP/DRP document is planned for 2026.  
Integration with incident management and monitoring tools is on the roadmap.

=== DOCUMENT 9 : Awareness & HR Security Notes ===
Version : 1.1 – February 2025  
Owner : HR Manager / Security Lead  
Language : French  

1. Objet  
---------
Décrire les pratiques de sensibilisation à la sécurité et les mesures RH appliquées au sein de CloudFirst.  
L’objectif est de favoriser une culture de sécurité minimale sans processus lourds.

2. Recrutement et intégration  
------------------------------
Les nouveaux collaborateurs signent une clause de confidentialité à l’embauche.  
Aucune vérification d’antécédents n’est réalisée de façon systématique ; elle dépend du poste et du bon vouloir du manager.  
Un onboarding technique inclut un rappel oral des règles de sécurité de base.

3. Sensibilisation  
-------------------
Une session de formation à la sécurité est organisée environ une fois par an sous forme de présentation interne.  
Le contenu est principalement orienté phishing et mots de passe.  
Aucun test d’évaluation ni suivi individuel n’est mis en place.

4. Comportements attendus  
--------------------------
Les employés doivent signaler toute anomalie via le canal Slack #security.  
Aucune procédure formelle de déclaration d’incident interne n’existe.  
Les sanctions disciplinaires potentielles ne sont pas définies.

5. Gestion des départs  
-----------------------
Lorsqu’un collaborateur quitte la société, son compte Google Workspace est désactivé sous 48 h.  
Les autres accès (GitHub, VPN) peuvent rester actifs plusieurs jours.  
Une procédure automatisée est à l’étude.

6. Améliorations prévues  
--------------------------
Mise en place d’un e-learning en 2026 et d’un audit RH annuel lié à la conformité SOC 2.

=== DOCUMENT 10 : Vendor Relationship & Subcontracting Policy ===
Version : 1.0 – November 2024  
Owner : Operations Lead  
Language : English  

1. Purpose  
-----------
This policy defines how CloudFirst manages relationships with third-party vendors and subcontractors.  
The goal is to maintain transparency and ensure minimal security due diligence despite limited resources.

2. Vendor Selection  
--------------------
Vendors are selected primarily based on price, functionality, and ease of integration.  
Security questionnaires are requested only for high-impact providers (e.g., hosting, payment, monitoring).  
No central vendor inventory exists; it is maintained in a shared spreadsheet.

3. Contractual Terms  
---------------------
Contracts include confidentiality clauses and standard Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) when provided by the vendor.  
CloudFirst does not yet have a formal template for security annexes.

4. Risk Assessment  
-------------------
A basic risk review is done before onboarding a new vendor, mostly focusing on availability and data-storage location.  
Findings are not formally documented.  
There is no periodic re-evaluation of vendor posture.

5. Monitoring  
--------------
If a vendor incident becomes public (e.g., via email alert or social media), the CTO reviews its impact.  
No automated threat-intelligence monitoring of vendors is performed.

6. Termination  
---------------
When a contract ends, credentials and API keys are revoked manually.  
Residual data deletion is requested from the vendor but not verified.

7. Review  
----------
The policy will be reviewed annually or when new compliance frameworks (SOC 2, ISO 27001) become relevant for CloudFirst.
